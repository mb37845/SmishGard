Learn About Smishing

Understand SMS phishing threats and how to protect yourself

What is Smishing?

Smishing (SMS phishing) is a type of cyberattack where criminals send fraudulent text messages to trick you into revealing personal information, clicking malicious links, or downloading harmful software.

🎯 Common Smishing Types

🏦 Bank Fraud

"Your account has been suspended. Click here to verify."

📦 Delivery Scams

"Your package can't be delivered. Update your address."

🎁 Prize Scams

"You've won a prize! Claim it now by clicking this link."

🔐 Account Alerts

"Your social media account needs verification."

💻 Tech Support Scams

"VIRUS DETECTED! Install this app immediately or call our support hotline to secure your device."

🚩 Smishing Red Flags

🔗
Suspicious URLs

Links that don't match the claimed organization

⏰
Urgency & Pressure

Messages creating false urgency to act quickly

💰
Financial Requests

Asking for banking or payment information

📱
Unknown Senders

Messages from numbers you don't recognize

🛡️ Protection Tips

Verify Directly

Contact organizations using official numbers/websites

Don't Click Links

Avoid clicking links in suspicious messages

Check URLs

Hover over links to see the actual destination

Use AI Tools

Leverage tools like SmishGuard for analysis

📝 Real Smishing Examples

🚩 Smishing

"ALERT: Your bank account has been locked. Call now to avoid closure."

Why it's suspicious: False urgency , financial threat
✅ Legitimate

"Amazon: Your package will arrive today by 8 PM. Track at: https://amazon.com/track/12345"

Why it's legitimate: Legitimate domain, specific tracking info, no urgency
